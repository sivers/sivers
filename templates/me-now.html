finished the videobook of “Useful Not True”

The very day I wrote the last word of “Useful Not True”, I went into my recording booth, and recorded the entire audiobook in one take, in 110 minutes.

But two months ago I listened to it again and realized I could do better. So I went back into the booth and turned on the camera as well, recording the audiobook while making a video at the same time.

Then I had to make subtitles, after aligning my transcript with the audio, which meant learning the Montreal Forced Aligner and the Sub Station Alpha subtitle format. Then I edited them by hand throughout to help emphasize the message of the book. The result is even better than the plain text book.

It’s available now at my store, sivers.com. If you already bought any digital format, it’s already yours.
